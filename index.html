Google Form Guideline

I followed the Google Form Guidelines for designing the Google Form.

  1. Communicate your purpose

In my form description, it’s clear what the data is being collected for, what the data will be used for, and it also explains that the form will be anonymous. To protect the privacy of the person filling out my form, I did not ask the personal questions, such as the age of the person.

  1. Only ask for data you actually need

All the questions revolved around my focus – Learning Mathematics, and there was nothing irrelevant to my topic.

  1. Consider your respondents

My form questions do not contain any sexist, racial discrimination and other uncomfortable content. The questions are not critical and people who fill out the form will not feel uncomfortable.

  1. Use validation rules

By setting up validation for form questions, I can ensure the data availability.

Questions with check boxes (fixed options) indicates that there may be more than one answer, and if the person who filling out the form selects both answers, Google Forms records them as two variables. In a “tidy” data, each row should have a unique observation and each column should record a unique variable.
